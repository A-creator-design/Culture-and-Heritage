Famous Temples of Arunachal Pradesh

Malinithan

Malinithan Temple is an ancient and sacred site located near Likabali in Arunachal Pradesh, at the foothills of the Siang Mountains. Believed to have been built between the 10th and 12th centuries, it is dedicated to Goddess Durga, worshipped here as Malini. The temple is renowned for its beautifully carved stone sculptures, including images of gods, goddesses, animals, and floral motifs. According to legend, Lord Krishna and Rukmini rested at this site during their journey, and Goddess Durga welcomed them, giving the place spiritual significance. Malinithan stands as a testament to early Hindu influence in the region and showcases the artistic and architectural brilliance of ancient Arunacha.

ParshuramKund

Parshuram Kund is a sacred pilgrimage site located on the banks of the Lohit River in the Lohit district of Arunachal Pradesh. It is associated with the legend of Lord Parshuram, the sixth incarnation of Lord Vishnu. According to mythology, Parshuram came here to atone for the sin of killing his mother at his father's command. He bathed in the holy waters of the Kund, which are believed to have washed away his sins. Every year, thousands of devotees visit the site, especially during Makar Sankranti, to take a holy dip in the Kund, believing it purifies their soul. Surrounded by rugged hills and lush forests, Parshuram Kund is not only a spiritual destination but also a spot of serene natural beauty and cultural significance.

Akashiganga

Akashiganga is a revered pilgrimage site located in the Upper Subansiri district of Arunachal Pradesh, known for its scenic beauty and spiritual significance. The name "Akashiganga," meaning "Sky Ganga," comes from the belief that the waters of the river are as sacred as the holy Ganga. According to local legends, it is believed that the water here descended from the heavens, making it a sacred place for bathing and offering prayers. The main attraction is a waterfall that cascades from a height, creating a breathtaking view as the water plunges into a pool below. Pilgrims and tourists alike visit Akashiganga not only for its natural beauty but also to partake in rituals and take a holy dip in the pristine waters. The site is surrounded by lush forests and the distant Himalayan peaks, providing a tranquil and spiritually enriching experience.
